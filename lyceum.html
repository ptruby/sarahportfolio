Use It or Lose It

Lyceum Fellowship Competition

Medical advancements have elongated the average lifespan, but leisure activities
are more difficult on the body and the alternatives serve as mindless diversions. Cities are one of the most effective environments to combat the tragedies of aging, yet they, too, are growing older, abandoned by businesses and residents. The Lyceum Fellowship Competition challenged students to redesign the Empire State Building for aging people in the aging city.

This solution focuses on imperceptibly keeping the body and mind healthy through
a variety of physical and mental activities through different scales: the city, the building, and the home. Each of these spaces has been transformed to effortlessly transform senior’s health.

For a closer view or to read the book please visit:

The video was a collaboration with Anjali Patel, Melissa Long, Ellen Crawford, and Chloe Bennett based on our Lyceum entries. The film was an entry to the Legacy Film Festival on Aging, which showcases films from around the world that celebrate older adulthood and deal with the challenges and triumphs of aging.
