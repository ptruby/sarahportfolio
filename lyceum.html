Lyceum Fellowship Competition

Medical advancements have elongated the average lifespan, but leisure activities are more difficult on the body and the alternatives serve as mindless diversions. Cities are one of the most effective environments to combat the tragedies of aging, yet they, too, are growing older, abandoned by businesses and residents. The Lyceum Fellowship Competition challenged students to redesign the Empire State Building for aging people in the aging city.

This solution focuses on imperceptibly keeping the body and mind healthy through a variety of physical and mental activites throughout the city, the building, and the home. Each of these spaces has been transformed to effortlessly transform senior’s health.

Programs used: Rhino, Hand Drawing, Adobe Photoshop
