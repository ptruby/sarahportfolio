Urban Farming + Smart Eating

One of the major issues in Cincinnati is the growing food desert, an area with little or no access to fresh and affordable foods needed to maintain a healthy diet. This access can be interpreted in several ways:

Physical access to shops — distance, hills, poor public transit, or no car. Healthy options may not be available.

Financial access — not enough money for healthier groceries, bus fare, grocery storage space. This limits individuals to cheaper local fast food outlets.

Knowledge of preparation — The consumer’s mental attitude or knowledge about nutrition and food preparation can be major barriers limiting access to fresh produce and other healthy food choices. Consumers may lack cooking knowledge or have the idea that eating a healthy diet is not important.

This Environmental Learning Center focuses on the healthy food production — growing, cooking, and eating. One can view all parts of the program from the primary promenade. Participants can also use the secondary promenade to get through the space designated by the greenhouse walls.
