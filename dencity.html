H2OPE

Dencity Competition

Rapid world growth and urbanization is not allowing cities to adapt and provide for their inhabitants. Some of the densest places in the world are comprised of makeshift homes, otherwise referred to as slums, and these overcrowded cities continue to grow. Slums effect much more than just housing; they affect almost all living conditions and communities as a whole. Shelter’s Dencity Competition challenges architects, planners, engineers, designers, and thinkers from all over the world to submit ideas on how to better handle the growing density of unplanned cities and to spread awareness of this massive problem, considering how design can empower communities and allow for a self sufficient future.

This solution focuses on empowering people through water in Africa’s Largest Slum, Kibera. There is a lack of clean, potable water available for reasonable prices in and around the slum. Our goal was to provide the infrastructure to not only collect and filter rainwater providing potable water to individual slum dwellers, but also provide a strong connection to the community economy using the water supply. This was a collaboration with Julie Morgan, Yi-Yun Lin, and Robert Piotrowski.
