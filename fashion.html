Fashion + Architecture

An interdisciplinary collaborative studio between fashion and architecture, this began as a way to discover the design processes that lay between architecture and design. This collaborative studio blended the inspirations (texture, graphics, draping, detail, and structure) of the Master of Architecture and Master of Design students
to create a student-designed collection built during the necessary iterative process.
My garment within the collection was a draped dress.

The second semester focused our inspiration on different methods of construction and fabrication, both additive and subtractive. Early experiments in cutting, acid-washing, plastering, and metal work led to a different exploration than the previous semester. I created a pattern to be laser-cut to highlight the wearer’s back.

Both pieces were featured in the 65th Annual DAAP Fashion Show.
