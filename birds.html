Winging It: An Experimental Gallery About Birds

A small group of five designers, three recent graduates, worked to design an exhibit for the Carnegie Museum of Natural History. The exhibit presents the ornithology department’s research in an interesting manner aimed at a college-aged audience.

We developed an exhibit aimed at college students to engage and educate them about bird research at the Carnegie Museum of Natural History in Pittsburgh. During this development we had to collaborate with museum researchers to understand the scope of subject matter and target population, recent high-school graduates, to understand the customer. To fully create the exhibit we designed and fabricated an exhibit that engaged and educated the customer, coming in on budget and on time.

We finished the exhibit in four months for less than $15,000. The exhibit, Winging It: An Experimental Gallery About Birds, opened September 23, 2010. Our exhibit, aimed at all audiences (we didn’t want to exclude others), received a positive review from all our colleges.
